Chilaquiles

Chilaquiles are a typical Mexican dish, It's ideal for your breakfast. Let's do it

Ingredients

  • 2 cups corn tortilla chips
  • 1 cup tomato sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup finely diced red onion
  • 2 beaten eggs
  • Pinch of salt
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Begin by heating up oil in a roomy, sturdy skillet to around 350°F (175°C). Fry up pieces of corn tortilla and diced onion in the hot oil until they turn crispy and acquire a beautiful golden-brown hue. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking. Once done, carefully transfer them onto a plate lined with paper towels to allow excess oil to drain. Empty the skillet, leaving only a faint trace of oil behind.
  2. Next, place the skillet over medium heat. Gently return the fried tortillas and onion to the skillet. Mix in gently beaten eggs, and season with a pinch of salt for flavor. Continue to cook and stir until the eggs set and form a delightful texture.
  3. Gradually introduce tomato sauce and water into the mixture, stirring skillfully to combine all the elements. Lower the heat to a gentle simmer and allow it to thicken naturally for approximately 10 minutes.
  4. As the final touch, sprinkle a layer of shredded cheese over the concoction. Continue cooking until the cheese melts gloriously, blanketing the dish with its gooey goodness.
